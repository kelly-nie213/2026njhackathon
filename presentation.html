Built for the ones without a security team

Who it's for

Small nonprofits — the hackathon's own Arc 4 customer. They hold sensitive data, get targeted constantly, and have zero budget for enterprise tools or staff to run them. Plus the everyday orgs and people who own a domain but can't read a DMARC record: churches, clubs, schools, small businesses.

Why now

AI now writes flawless, personalized phishing at scale. The cost of attacking the smallest, least-defended targets just dropped to near zero — so they've become the easiest and most profitable ones to hit. The orgs with the least defense are suddenly the most exposed.

Is the approach fresh — or a clone of what already exists?

Honest tools, built for them

Real, never guessed

Every finding comes from a live source. Sources we can't reach show "not checked" — never a fake all-clear. No LLM hallucinating a risk score. The core runs with no API key, so it's free to operate.

For the non-technical

Most tools are dashboards for an IT team that doesn't exist here. Aegis gives plain-English, prioritized, role-aware steps — and an advisor that answers in their words and budget.

The fresh part · Verifiable Security Badge

A nonprofit can prove an independent Aegis scan graded their site — without publishing the report, which would hand attackers a roadmap. Each claim is hashed into a Merkle tree and signed; the public badge reveals only what they choose (domain, grade, dates) and anyone can verify it offline. Selective disclosure, built for trust without exposure.
